Digital Tech

Outcomes

  • 1.1 The Purpose of IT in Modern Business - The digital world is changing at an increasing rate. IT is no longer considered a single department within an organisation and 34% of companies reportedly see digital technology as the main driver of innovation1 (ref-Forbes or Accenture). It is considered an aid not only to marketing, but to competitive advantage2 (Forbes). The fast acquisition, processing and utilisation of information within business3 (bizFluent) is essential to the modern business strategy, but failure to embrace new technologies in the right way presents security risks.

  • 1.2 Methods, Skills and Resources - Successful digital projects can require a wide range of skills, including an understanding of, for example, account management, strategy and business analysis4 (Digital Project Manager). As well as the more obvious digital platforms, it is also important to have a strong grasp of project management software and content management systems5 (Workamajig). Moreover, it is essential to have the ability to collaborate with and manage people. The digital landscape is a dynamic one and it is important to continue to engage in the community to take advantage of new resources and good practice.

  • 1.3 Planning and Acting with IT- IT is an invaluable tool in organising and tracking projects. In building a website I have begun to incorporate Trello into my processes. This allows me to define and divide tasks into an accessible ‘board’ format by which to manage my time. (link!)
